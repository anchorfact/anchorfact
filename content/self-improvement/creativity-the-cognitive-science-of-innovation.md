## TL;DR

Creativity involves two cognitive modes: divergent thinking (generating many ideas, associated with the default mode network) and convergent thinking (evaluating and refining, associated with the executive control network). The creative process alternates between these modes. Contrary to the "lone genius" myth, creativity is domain-specific (expertise in a field is prerequisite) and thrives in environments balancing psychological safety with intellectual challenge.

## Core Explanation

Key models: (1) Wallas's 4-stage model (1926): preparation → incubation → illumination → verification, supported by neuroscience showing default mode network activation during incubation, (2) Amabile's Componential Theory of Creativity — domain-relevant skills, creativity-relevant processes, task motivation, and social environment, (3) Csikszentmihalyi's Systems Model — creativity emerges from the interaction of domain (culture), field (gatekeepers), and person. The 10,000-hour rule (Ericsson 1993) applies to creative expertise. Brainstorming effectiveness: nominal groups (individuals generating ideas separately then pooling) produce 2x more and 2x better ideas than face-to-face groups (Diehl & Stroebe 1987). Constraints paradoxically enhance creativity — "necessity is the mother of invention" has empirical support. Key environmental factors: autonomy, resources, encouragement, and moderate pressure.
