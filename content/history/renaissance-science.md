## TL;DR

Renaissance science grew out of closer observation, mathematical description, new instruments, and faster circulation of printed knowledge. Figures such as Copernicus, Vesalius, and Galileo helped shift European natural philosophy away from inherited authority toward evidence-based inquiry.

## Core Explanation

Vesalius's 1543 anatomy work emphasized direct human dissection. Galileo's telescopic observations, including Jupiter's moons and the phases of Venus, challenged geocentric models and supported the broader Copernican shift. Renaissance humanism and print culture helped make observation, comparison, and debate central to scientific practice.
