## TL;DR
Game balance fundamentals are about tuning rules, resources, rewards, risks, pacing, difficulty, and player choices so a game supports its intended experience. Balance is not one universal midpoint; it depends on audience, genre, goals, and play context.

## Core Explanation
Competitive games often emphasize fairness and counterplay. Single-player games may balance challenge curves, resource scarcity, and pacing. Cooperative games may balance roles and contribution. Designers use prototypes, playtests, data, and qualitative feedback to find whether mechanics are producing the intended experience rather than relying on raw numerical symmetry alone.
